# Rotating Two-Seat Bench Requirements

## Concept

The vehicle interior uses a two-seat bench that should work in both living and driving modes.

## Required Positions

### 0 degrees - living / dining position

The bench is positioned along the side wall of the vehicle and faces the table. In this position, the bench should sit as close to the side wall as possible to keep the interior space open and comfortable.

### -90 degrees - driving position

The same bench rotates by 90 degrees so passengers sit facing forward, in the direction of travel. In this position, the bench must be securely locked and suitable for homologated passenger use in an M1 camper van.

### +90 degrees - optional living / dining position

An additional opposite 90-degree position would be useful, but it is not mandatory.

## Movement Requirement

Because the bench sits close to the side wall in the living position, a simple central rotation is probably not sufficient.

Preferred movement:

1. Unlock the bench.
2. Slide it away from the side wall.
3. Rotate it by 90 degrees.
4. Lock it securely in the forward-facing driving position.

## Safety and Homologation

The driving position must be suitable for M1 passenger transport.

Important requirements:

- secure locking in the driving position
- documentation for homologation / approval
- preferably integrated 3-point seat belts
- compact design suitable for a premium camper van interior
- lightweight but robust construction

For the living / dining position, light mechanical retention or positioning is desirable for comfort and usability, but it does not necessarily need to be certified as a driving position.

## Known Rough Cost / Weight Feedback

From supplier discussion:

- M1 testing estimate: approximately 30,000 to 60,000 EUR depending on vehicle, bench production, test preparation, and related work
- estimated bench weight: approximately 80 to 95 kg per bench
- rough bench budget: approximately 2,000 EUR per bench

These figures are preliminary and require confirmation.
