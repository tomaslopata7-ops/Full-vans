# Folding Lift Bed Concept

## Target

Create a camper van bed that can fold approximately in the middle and lift one half upward toward the ceiling when not in use.

Current project layout:

- total bed size approximately 1800 x 2000 mm
- fixed half above the cab area
- moving half above the seating and dining table area
- moving half approximately 1800 x 1000 mm
- front half lifts upward toward the ceiling

Main goals:

- preserve comfortable sleeping surface when folded down
- free usable interior space when lifted
- keep the mechanism simple, robust and serviceable
- secure the bed safely for driving
- avoid heavy or complex electric lift systems unless required later

## Recommended Concept

Use a two-part bed platform:

1. Fixed or semi-fixed rear platform section.
2. Hinged front platform section that folds upward toward the ceiling.

The hinge line should run across the width of the bed. The lifted half should be supported by gas struts, locking stays or a mechanical latch system.

## Mechanical Layout

### Bed Platform

Recommended construction:

- aluminium rectangular tube perimeter frame
- lightweight plywood, honeycomb panel or composite board as platform surface
- mattress split at the same line as the bed platform
- anti-slip / retaining detail for the mattress when the lifted half is raised

The two platform halves should meet on a supported edge when in sleeping position, so the hinge is not the only element carrying sleeping load.

### Hinge

Recommended options:

- continuous stainless piano hinge across the full width
- several heavy-duty stainless hinges with through-bolted mounting plates
- marine pullman / bunk-bed hinge mechanism if a more integrated solution is desired

Avoid relying only on small furniture hinges. The hinge must handle dynamic vehicle loads and the weight of the mattress.

### Lift Assistance

Recommended options:

- two gas struts, one on each side
- locking gas struts if the raised bed must hold securely without a separate latch
- mechanical locking stays if gas strut tuning becomes difficult

Gas strut force must be calculated after the approximate weight of the moving bed half and mattress is known. The mounting geometry is as important as the nominal strut force.

### Raised Position

The raised half should be secured mechanically:

- positive latch to wall / roof structure
- secondary safety strap or catch
- soft stops / bumpers at the upper position

Do not rely only on gas struts for travel locking.

### Sleeping Position

The lowered bed must sit on solid supports:

- side ledgers / rails
- front cross support
- locking pins or draw latches to prevent movement
- anti-rattle pads

The hinge should guide the movement, but the support rails should carry most of the sleeping load.

## Key Design Questions

- Bed width and length.
- Which half should lift: front half, rear half or side half.
- Weight of moving platform half.
- Mattress type, thickness and whether it is split.
- Available height to the ceiling.
- Available side wall structure for gas strut brackets.
- Whether the lifted section must clear cabinets, shower module or seats.
- Required sleeping load target.

## Preliminary Recommendation

For the first prototype, build a manual lift system:

1. Two-part aluminium bed frame.
2. Full-width piano hinge at the folding line.
3. Split mattress.
4. Two side-mounted gas struts.
5. Mechanical upper latch for travel.
6. Solid lower supports so the hinge does not carry sleeping load.

This is simpler and lighter than a full drop-down electric bed, while still giving a useful space-saving function.

## Project-Specific Recommendation

For the current 1800 x 2000 mm bed layout, the preferred concept is:

1. Fixed rear bed half above the cab.
2. Hinged front bed half above the seating / dining area.
3. Hinge line across the full 1800 mm width.
4. Moving half lifts upward and slightly forward / upward depending on available ceiling shape.
5. Two gas struts or locking gas struts mounted near the side walls.
6. Two positive upper latches mounted into structural side-wall / roof reinforcement.
7. Lower support rails and locking pins in sleeping position.

Estimated moving-half weight for early sizing:

- lightweight aluminium/composite frame: approximately 12-20 kg
- platform board / slats: approximately 6-12 kg
- half mattress: approximately 8-18 kg
- bedding / trim / hardware: approximately 3-8 kg

Expected moving-half total: approximately 30-55 kg.

This estimate must be confirmed by weighing the real prototype parts before final gas strut selection.

## Retention / Locking Concept

### Upper Position

The raised half should be captured at two points, left and right, ideally into metal brackets connected to the vehicle body or a reinforced furniture frame tied into the body.

Recommended latch types:

- over-centre draw latch with safety catch
- spring-loaded locking pin
- rotary compression latch
- secondary safety strap as backup

Do not let the raised bed rely only on gas struts during driving.

### Lower / Sleeping Position

In sleeping position, the moving half should sit on:

- continuous side support rails
- front cross support or two local support blocks
- anti-rattle rubber pads
- mechanical locking pins or draw latches to stop bouncing during driving

The hinge should locate and rotate the moving half, but the sleeping load should go into the side rails and cross supports.

## Supplier / Hardware Directions

### Gas struts

Look for:

- bed lift gas struts
- RV / marine hatch gas struts
- locking gas struts for safety-critical holding positions
- force range selected after weighing the moving bed half

Candidate suppliers / products:

- Stabilus LIFT-O-MAT gas springs, including versions with end-position locking
- Stabilus BLOC-O-LIFT / KOMBI-LIFT lockable gas springs for controlled positioning
- Bansbach easylift custom gas springs
- generic RV / marine hatch gas struts for prototype trials

### Complete bed lift systems

Candidate systems:

- Lippert / Project 2000 EuroLoft or Smart Bed systems
- Häfele Teleletto foldaway bed fittings
- Häfele PRATIK slatted frame lift mechanisms

Notes:

- Lippert / Project 2000 is a real RV bed-lift direction, but it is more of a complete lifting bed system than a simple hinged half-bed.
- Häfele products are furniture-grade and useful for hardware reference, but must be validated carefully for vehicle vibration and travel locking.
- For this project, a custom hinged frame with gas struts may be simpler and cleaner than adapting a domestic wall-bed mechanism.

### Aluminium bed frame manufacturers / suppliers

Candidate suppliers:

- Mobiframe - European camper conversion equipment manufacturer with aluminium beds in the product range.
- Inducamp / Inducomp B.V. - Dutch supplier of custom aluminium camper interiors and loose aluminium frames for beds, kitchens and storage systems.
- LETTO VAN - Italian modular van bed in aluminium and wood; can make custom beds for different vans.
- Vansy - German modular camper bed modules, useful as a reference for lightweight modular construction.
- Lippert / Project 2000 - RV bed systems and aluminium-frame van bed concepts, more suitable for complete OEM-style systems.

Best fit for this project:

1. Inducamp / Inducomp if a custom aluminium frame supplier is needed.
2. Mobiframe if an existing camper-specific aluminium bed product can be adapted.
3. LETTO VAN or Vansy as lightweight modular references, but probably not directly suitable for the hinged ceiling-lift concept without custom work.

### Hinges

Look for:

- stainless piano hinge
- heavy-duty marine hinge
- pullman bed hinge
- bunk bed hinge mechanism

### Latches

Look for:

- positive locking latch
- over-centre latch
- spring-loaded locking pin
- anti-rattle latch

## Notes

Commercial camper and RV bed systems often use gas-piston lift assistance and adjustable latches. Marine pullman bed mechanisms are also relevant because they are designed for moving environments and compact interiors.
